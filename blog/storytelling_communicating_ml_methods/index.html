Decision Trees:

Decision Trees is much like the “Who am I?” game.
If i am asked to explain decision trees to non-statisticans I like to let them play a game of “Who am I?” and let them do guess role with side rule to take as least guesses as possible. Intuitively they will try to find questions that rule out 50% of the remaining “population” (as this is the max that can) be ruled out per question.

Lets assume you play that game with your nephew and he is really into animals. He thinks of one and you have to find out whether he is a sea animal, bird or “land” animal. After the 10th round you get a bit bored so you decide you need a program that will ask questions for you. This program will be very similar to a decision tree.

Guessing Game: Guess which playing card am I thinking of? (Least amount of questions win)

Similar game: Ask the right questions to determine if a product will be a success? Ask the right questions to determine if a customer should be awarded discount?
